# Thesis

Prevent significant engineering effort from being spent on the wrong product.

Research current facts and alternatives before asking. Ask only for goals, constraints, decisions, or unavailable first-hand knowledge, one material question at a time.

Maintain:

| Hypothesis | Evidence | Status |

Establish:

1. recurring frustration and affected customer;
2. outcome-oriented job and current workflow;
3. existing alternatives and commoditised capabilities;
4. an unowned job, not merely a missing feature;
5. core verb, core object, differentiation, and why now;
6. smallest valuable experience and trust boundary.

Stress-test against incumbent expansion, stronger models, cheaper execution, platform restrictions, and available integrations.

Evidence moves the thesis. Update the register and revisit affected conclusions when evidence changes. Do not design architecture before the opportunity and smallest product are defensible.

Return exactly one:

- Proceed
- Proceed but Narrow
- Pivot
- Research Further
- Stop

Include the decisive evidence, changed assumption, biggest unknown, and one next action.
